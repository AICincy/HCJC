The Hamilton County Court System
Which court does what, where to find it, who presides · ORC § 149.45 transparency

Most people who land on JCStream don't know which court will hear their case or their loved one's case. Hamilton County has six courts with overlapping geography and very different jurisdictions. This page mirrors the published information from hamiltoncountycourts.org, probatect.org, and the Clerk of Courts so the distinctions are in one place. Public records belong to the people; officials act as trustees.

Scam alert (issued by the Court of Common Pleas) Scammers actively impersonate Hamilton County Sheriff's Office, Electronic Monitoring Unit, and Clerk of Courts personnel, calling families of incarcerated individuals and demanding fees for "release via electronic GPS ankle monitor." They use spoofed numbers and publicly available arrest records, and request payment via Zelle, Venmo, or Cash App. Hamilton County will never require payment for electronic monitoring before release from the Justice Center. Verify directly with the Electronic Monitoring Unit at 513-946-9800.

Court of Common Pleas, General Division

The trial court of general jurisdiction. Hears felony criminal cases and civil cases in which the amount in controversy is more than $15,000, including matters involving title to real estate (e.g. foreclosures). Approximately 16 judges; cases are assigned to a single judge by lot at filing and stay with that judge through termination.

Hamilton County Municipal Court

Handles misdemeanor criminal cases, traffic offenses, and civil cases in which the amount in controversy is less than $15,000, including small claims and landlord-tenant matters. Also conducts preliminary hearings for felony cases before they transfer to Common Pleas, and oversees probation for misdemeanor offenders. The court operates several specialty divisions (housing, environmental, drug court, mental health court) and a Driver's License Intervention Program.

Court of Common Pleas, Domestic Relations Division

Handles divorce, dissolution of marriage, legal separation, annulment, custody between parents, spousal support, and parental rights. Distinct from Juvenile Court (custody between a parent and a non-parent goes to Juvenile). Cases are assigned by lot to a judge at filing and stay with that judge.

First District Court of Appeals

The intermediate appellate court for Hamilton County. Hamilton County is the only county within the First District; the surrounding counties (Brown, Butler, Clermont, Clinton, Fayette, Madison, Preble, Warren) are in the Twelfth District. Reviews appeals from Common Pleas (all divisions) and Municipal Court. Three-judge panels hear each case; decisions from the panel can be appealed to the Ohio Supreme Court.

Mayor's Courts in Hamilton County

Ohio is one of only two states (the other is Louisiana) that allows the mayors of municipal corporations to preside over a court. In Hamilton County municipalities populated by more than 100 people with no municipal court of their own, Mayor's Courts hear cases involving local ordinance violations and state traffic laws. A mayor is not required to be a lawyer; they may appoint an attorney with at least three years of practice to preside. A person convicted in Mayor's Court may appeal to the Hamilton County Municipal Court.

How a Case Moves Through the System

An adult felony arrest in Hamilton County typically follows this path. The path for a misdemeanor differs at step 2 onward (it stays in Municipal Court).

  • Arrest by HCSO, Cincinnati PD, or another local jurisdiction. Booking at the Justice Center, 1000 Sycamore Street.
  • Arraignment in Hamilton County Municipal Court (Room 205) within a short time after booking. The judge sets bond and reads the charges. For misdemeanors and traffic, the case stays in Municipal Court; for felonies, this is a preliminary hearing.
  • If a felony, the case is bound over to the Court of Common Pleas, General Division (Room 410) after a preliminary hearing in Municipal Court or after a grand jury indictment.
  • Case is assigned by lot to one Common Pleas judge under the Individual Assignment System (Local Rule 7). That judge handles every motion, hearing, plea, and trial in the case.
  • Within 90 days of filing (120 days for foreclosure cases), the assigned judge holds a Case Management Conference to schedule the rest of the case (Local Rule 15).
  • Resolution: plea, trial verdict, dismissal, or transfer to a specialty court (drug court, mental health court, Veterans Treatment Court).
  • Sentence: probation, fines, restitution, county jail (typically misdemeanor), state prison (felonies). For diversion or treatment court, supervised release with treatment conditions.
  • Appeal: from Common Pleas or Municipal Court, to the First District Court of Appeals (12th floor, William Howard Taft Law Center). From there, by leave, to the Ohio Supreme Court.
