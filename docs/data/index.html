Data, Methodology & Legal Notices
Open data feeds, licensing, and the rules of the road for this site

JCStream is generated exclusively from public records maintained by the Hamilton County Sheriff's Office (HCSO) and published through HCSO's public inmate-search portal at hcso.org. Everything the site is built from is published here as plain JSON, rebuilt about every 30 minutes by GitHub Actions. We mirror the current roster: when HCSO removes a person, the next sweep removes them here too. There is no historical archive of released individuals.

How it’s built

A GitHub Actions cron sweeps hcso.org’s inmate search (substring match on last name — the 26 single letters cover the whole roster with dedup), parses each detail page, downscales the photo, diffs against the prior snapshot, pulls the Cincinnati Open Data feeds, regenerates this site, and commits. Source: github.com/AICincy/JCStream.

Crowdsourced courtclerk.org data

JCStream does not scrape courtclerk.org. The Hamilton County Clerk of Courts explicitly disallows automated access to /data/ in their robots.txt, and the prior maintainer of this project made the principled call to respect that limit. A scraper here would also break every time the Clerk's site changes.

But there's a path that works: any reader who looks up a case at courtclerk.org (passing their bot check in a regular browser) can paste the docket fields into a one-minute GitHub form. The form's a structured issue template; a workflow ingests it into data/courtclerk_cases.json; the next site build then displays the submission on the matching inmate's detail page under "Submitted by readers." Anyone reading that inmate's page sees the court information without anyone scraping anyone.

This is a deliberate trade: coverage will always be patchy, because only what someone bothers to submit appears. The upside: every entry was looked up by a real person, so the data quality on what does come in is higher than any scraper would manage.

Dispatch correlation (researcher mode)

Cincinnati Open Data publishes calls-for-service rows with disposition codes (arrest, citation, offense report) but no inmate identifier. Hamilton County publishes the booking record but no link back to the originating dispatch. The two databases are public; the join is not.

dispatch_correlations.json attempts the join automatically and outputs candidate pairs by inmate_number and CFS row index, with a confidence score weighted 50/50 between temporal proximity (within a 60-minute window) and textual overlap between the booking's charge description and the CFS row's disposition/incident-type fields. Pairs below 0.35 confidence are dropped.

JCStream does not publish these joins on its own pages. The file is intentionally a raw data export. A high-confidence pair still requires human verification before any public claim is made that a specific dispatch led to a specific arrest. The defamation risk of automated false-positives is real; the researcher-mode export delegates that judgment to whoever's actually doing the reporting.

Anonymized event log (permanent)

The roster mirror only keeps individuals while they're on the public roster: when HCSO releases someone, the next sweep removes them here. That's the privacy default, and it stays.

But aggregate counts shouldn't disappear too. anon_changelog.json is a parallel feed that retains every booking and release event forever, with all identifying fields (name, inmate_number, booking_number) stripped after 7 days. What survives the strip is the event type, the date (day-precision, not minute), the offense tier (F1-MM), and the primary charge category. That's enough to answer institutional-pattern questions ("how does Friday booking volume compare to Tuesday over the last year?", "what was the F1 share of the roster during summer 2025?") without keeping anyone individually visible after they've left custody.

Legal notices

Presumption of innocence

Every individual listed on this site is legally presumed innocent unless and until proven guilty in a court of law. An arrest or booking record is not evidence of guilt. Charges may be reduced, dismissed, or result in acquittal. This site does not editorialize about guilt or innocence.

Legal authority

JCStream is generated exclusively from public records maintained by the Hamilton County Sheriff’s Office and published through HCSO’s public inmate-search portal. The right of public access to these records is established by the Ohio Public Records Act, ORC § 149.43, which provides that public records be open and available for inspection.

Record removal & sealing compliance

When HCSO removes a record from its public inmate roster, this site removes the corresponding record on its next automated update cycle, typically within 30 minutes. This site does not maintain a historical archive of removed records. For records subject to a court order of sealing or expungement under ORC § 2953.32 (as amended — including changes by 135th G.A. HB 234 and 136th G.A. HB 96), this site will remove the record upon receiving notice of the order. To submit notice, or to report any error, open an issue at github.com/AICincy/JCStream/issues. Removal and correction requests are processed at no cost — there is never a fee, and there never will be. This site’s voluntary compliance with sealing and expungement orders reflects its respect for the rehabilitative purpose of Ohio’s sealing and expungement statutes (ORC §§ 2953.31–2953.61).

Fair Credit Reporting Act notice

This site is not a consumer reporting agency as defined by the Fair Credit Reporting Act, 15 U.S.C. § 1681 et seq. The information here has not been collected, in whole or in part, for the purpose of furnishing consumer reports. You may not use this site to make decisions about consumer credit, employment, insurance, housing, tenant screening, or any other purpose that would require compliance with the FCRA.

Purpose

JCStream exists to increase public access to, and transparency of, Hamilton County’s criminal justice system. By republishing HCSO’s public inmate roster in a structured, searchable format, this project supports civic awareness, journalistic inquiry, court-watching, and public accountability. It does not editorialize about guilt or innocence, does not accept payment for record modification or removal, and removes records when the source agency removes them.

No fees — no exceptions

This site will never charge any person for the review, correction, or removal of their record. If you encounter any third party claiming to offer paid removal services for records on this site, that party is not affiliated with JCStream and cannot fulfill that promise. Removal requests are processed at github.com/AICincy/JCStream/issues at no cost.
