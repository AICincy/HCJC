# Research Execution - Source Hierarchy Reference

**Last updated:** May 6, 2026

This hierarchy governs source selection for research briefs. Higher tiers preempt lower tiers when both are available. Lower tiers are admissible when higher tiers are silent or unavailable.

## Tier 1 - Primary Authority

| Domain | Source |
|--------|--------|
| Federal statutes | uscode.house.gov (Office of the Law Revision Counsel) |
| Federal regulations | ecfr.gov (Electronic Code of Federal Regulations) and federalregister.gov |
| Federal case law | Issuing court's official opinion repository or PACER docket |
| State statutes (Ohio) | codes.ohio.gov/ohio-revised-code |
| State regulations (Ohio) | codes.ohio.gov/ohio-administrative-code |
| State case law (Ohio) | sconet.state.oh.us (Ohio Supreme Court) and ohiocourtsofappeals.com |
| Federal agency guidance | The issuing agency's official document repository (e.g., hhs.gov/hipaa, cms.gov) |
| Standards bodies | The issuing organization's published standard (e.g., HL7.org, ISO.org) |
| Scientific literature | Peer-reviewed journals indexed in PubMed; preprints from arXiv/bioRxiv with caveat |
| Statistical data | The issuing agency's data repository (e.g., census.gov, bls.gov, cdc.gov) |

Tier 1 sources are authoritative. Cite them directly with full pin citation when available.

## Tier 2 - Secondary Authority

| Domain | Source |
|--------|--------|
| Legal aggregators | CourtListener, Justia, Casetext (free); Westlaw, LexisNexis (subscription) |
| Regulatory news | National Law Review, JD Supra, Law360 |
| Medical guideline aggregators | National Guideline Clearinghouse archives, specialty society websites |
| Scientific aggregators | Google Scholar, Semantic Scholar |
| News (verified) | AP, Reuters, major metropolitan newspapers, BBC, NPR |

Tier 2 sources are useful for navigation and survey. Cite them only when Tier 1 is unavailable, and label the citation as secondary.

## Tier 3 - Reference and Synthesis

| Domain | Source |
|--------|--------|
| Encyclopedias | Wikipedia (with date-of-access caveat); Encyclopedia of Law and Society |
| Treatises and textbooks | Named authors with established credentials in the field |
| Practice guides | Bar association publications, professional society treatises |
| Commercial databases | Industry-standard databases relevant to the field |

Tier 3 sources are starting points, not endpoints. Use them to identify Tier 1 and Tier 2 sources to consult directly.

## Tier 4 - Last Resort

| Domain | Source |
|--------|--------|
| Forums | Stack Exchange family, specialty subreddits, professional forums |
| Blogs | Practitioner blogs with named authorship |
| Aggregator services | Q&A platforms, generic content aggregators |

Tier 4 sources are admissible only when Tiers 1-3 are silent. Always corroborate Tier 4 claims against a higher tier before relying on them in a brief.

## Inadmissible Sources

The following are not cited in research briefs under any circumstances:

- Anonymous content with no author attribution
- Generative AI output without independent verification
- Content from sites known to host fabricated material
- Aggregators that scrape and republish without attribution
- Marketing material disguised as research

## Recency Rules

| Topic class | Acceptable source age |
|-------------|-----------------------|
| Pure mathematics, classical physics, established history | No age limit |
| General medical knowledge | 5 years; older for foundational principles |
| Legal authority | Current version only; verify against the canonical Tier 1 source |
| Software, technology, AI | 12 months for active topics; 24 months for stable topics |
| Statistics and demographics | Most recent published edition |
| News and current events | 30 days for active stories |

When the question is time-sensitive, search for content within the relevant recency window before consulting older sources. Note the source date in the brief.

## Source Diversity

Research briefs should cite multiple sources across multiple tiers when the question permits. A brief that rests on a single Tier 2 source for a contested question is not a brief; it is a forwarded link.

For decisions of consequence (legal filings, medical determinations, financial commitments), require at least one Tier 1 source per claim. For survey or background briefs, multiple Tier 2 and Tier 3 sources are sufficient.
