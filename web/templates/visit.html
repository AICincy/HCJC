Visiting Someone at the Justice Center
Hamilton County Justice Center · 1000 Sycamore Street, Cincinnati, Ohio 45202

JCStream does not handle visitation. This page reproduces the published Hamilton County Sheriff's Office (HCSO) policy and contact information so family members who arrive here looking for the actual policy don't have to hunt for it. The HCSO page is the source of record →

Scam alert: electronic monitoring "release fees" Hamilton County will never call you, or your incarcerated loved one's family, demanding a fee for an electronic GPS ankle monitor before release. The Hamilton County Court of Common Pleas has issued a public warning that scammers impersonate the Sheriff's Office, the Electronic Monitoring Unit (EMU), and the Clerk of Courts. They use publicly available arrest records and demand non-traceable payments via Zelle, Venmo, or Cash App. If you receive one of these calls, hang up and verify with the EMU directly at 513-946-9800.

Rules HCSO Enforces

  • The registered visitor must be present at the time of the visit.
  • Visitors must be appropriately dressed for the entirety of the visit. Absolutely no nudity of any kind.
  • No illicit or lewd behavior. No weapons or dangerous ordnance displayed during the visit.
  • Individuals must be more than 30 days from their release date to be visitable.
  • No active temporary protection orders (TPOs) against the inmate naming the visitor.
  • No active warrants out for the visitor.
  • Valid government-issued photo ID is required to register and to visit.
  • No minors without an adult present. Maximum four visitors at one time.
  • The account must include a photo of the visitor's government ID, date of birth, ID number, and expiration date.
  • Profile pictures cannot contain any social-media filters.
  • Violation of any rule results in immediate termination of the visit without credit and a potential ban from all future visits.

What JCStream Will Never Publish

By policy, even when the underlying data may technically be public. See data & legal for the full scope statement.

  • Identities, addresses, or phone numbers of visitors.
  • Phone-call records, transcripts, or recordings.
  • Commissary purchases, deposit amounts, or money transfers.
  • Internal classification (gang affiliation, security level, behavior notes).
  • Medical, psychiatric, or program-participation information.
  • Identifying information about minors, victims, or witnesses.

If a Record on This Site Is Causing You Harm

Records here come and go automatically: when HCSO removes a person from the public roster, that record drops from JCStream on the next sweep (about every 30 minutes). For earlier removal, for example after a court order of sealing or expungement under ORC § 2953.32, open an issue at github.com/AICincy/JCStream/issues.

There is never a fee, and there never will be.

Not a consumer reporting agency. Do not use this site for employment, housing, credit, insurance, tenant screening, or any purpose governed by the Fair Credit Reporting Act, 15 U.S.C. § 1681 et seq.
